ORGANIZATION OF THE WEEK: NEW PROVIDENCE HISTORICAL SOCIETY

Western Union County Organization Spotlight Team, September 11, 2020


New Providence Historical Society Organization: New Providence Historical Society Organization formed in 1966 Mission Statement – The mission of the New Providence Historical Society is to educate the public about the local American experience through a generational perspective. We illuminate the past through student programs, museum tours and archival access. Society maintains two facilities in town- Salt Box Museum, 1350 Springfield Ave. – Historic building, dating back to late 1700’s; restored as a mid-1800’s farmhouse, with 4 first floor rooms open for tours. Typically open to public, first Sunday of each month. The open lawn area of this facility has been the site of annual Craft Fairs and N.P. Book Festivals. Mason Room Archives, 377 Elkwood Ave.(rear of N.P. Memorial Library, entry via center isle of library). Various archival material; photographs, maps, documents, transcribed oral interviews, paintings, and bound newspapers, pertaining to the local history of the Borough of New Providence. Open to public each Tuesday, 10 a.m. – noon; and by request. Phone: 908 665-1034 Website : newprovidencehistorical.com (email contact to society via website) Facebook : NewProvidenceHistoricalSociety The society holds monthly business meetings at the Mason Room open to public; an annual Dinner/Program each April, at a local restaurant; and a fall program on a historical subject, open to the public. We also take part in the Union County “Four Centuries in a Weekend Event”, held each October, the N.P Street Fair., and we took part in the N.P. Tricentennial Kickoff Event, February 2020. The society invites residents to visit our facilities. We also have an informative “Images of America” – New Providence book available for purchase; containing numerous archival photos of town. Book available at both Salt Box and Mason Room.(makes nice gift!). We invite residents to join our society, become an active participant, or support our mission with a membership donation. We welcome residents to volunteer in numerous ways, as a museum docent, in our archive room, taking selective photos of our town, setting up historical library displays, publicizing our society’s events, or supporting our various events throughout the year, with a helping hand.
